# Essay Writing Guide

How every Veritas essay is written. Each published essay has **three versions** of the same ideas — only the presentation changes, never the core argument.

- **Normal** — the standard read for everybody (this is the default tab).
- **Advanced** — an academic read for scholars and serious students.
- **Beginner** — a gentle read for someone new to the Bible.

---

## Golden rule: British spelling throughout

Write in **British English** across all three versions. This is about *spelling*, not tone — do not affect a "British voice" or accent. Just use British forms of words.

- Use `-ise` / `-isation`, not `-ize` / `-ization` (realise, organise, emphasise, civilisation).
- Use `-our`, not `-or` (colour, honour, behaviour, favour, Saviour).
- Use `-re`, not `-er` (centre, theatre).
- Use `-lled` / `-lling` (travelled, modelling).
- Other British forms: defence, offence, practise (verb), grey, judgement, towards, learnt.

Keep direct Bible quotations exactly as they appear in the source translation, even if the spelling differs.

---

## Punctuation: avoid the em dash

Do not use the em dash (the long dash `—`) to break up sentences. Rewrite instead.

Use one of these in its place:

- A **comma**, where the aside is light (`He gave them pictures, not because He needed them, but because they did.`).
- A **full stop**, to split into two sentences where the thought is strong enough to stand alone.
- A **colon**, where the second part explains or expands the first.
- **Brackets (parentheses)**, for a genuine aside.

This keeps the prose clean and readable rather than leaning on dashes. (Hyphens in normal compound words, like "book-by-book", are fine. This rule is only about the long sentence-breaking dash.)


---

## Step 1 — Write the Normal version first

Always start here. **Just write the essay.**

- Do not think about levels, tabs, or audiences yet.
- Do not worry about how it will be placed or how it sounds.
- Write the argument freely and naturally, start to finish.

The Normal version is the essay in its honest, natural form. The other two versions are *adaptations* of it — so it must exist first.

---

## Step 2 — Write the Advanced version

The Advanced version is the same essay presented as a more **academic, factual, educational read** — written as though it were published in a scholarly journal, for a reader who is a scholar.

It carries the **same ideas** as the Normal version. What changes:

- More formal, precise, structured tone.
- Provides background, context, and the supporting verses explicitly.
- Reads like serious study material rather than reflection.

**Before writing it, go back and re-read the resource materials.** The Advanced version is where we add depth, so we must check we are not missing additional evidential lines — extra verses, extra examples, extra supporting points that belong in a scholarly treatment.

Resource materials to read back through:

- **The books** — `books/` (e.g. *Conversations About God*, *Servants or Friends*), `Picture-of-God-Book/`, `grahams-dessertation.txt/`.
- **The raw Bible-series text** — `raw_text/textfiles/` (the book-by-book source for the specific books the essay discusses).
- **The AI summaries** — `AI interpretations/Haiku/` (the per-book summaries).
- **The greater principles** — `AI interpretations/Haiku/broader-concepts.md`.

While re-reading, look specifically for:

- Additional supporting verses to cite.
- Extra evidential angles or examples not used in the Normal version.
- Background and context that strengthens the argument for a scholarly reader.

---

## Step 3 — Write the Beginner version

The Beginner version is a **watered-down** version of the same essay, for someone who is **not familiar with the Bible** — a young person, someone new to the faith, or someone who simply has not read the stories.

Assume **no familiarity with the stories or the characters.** This is the key difference.

- Explain who's who and what is going on in each story.
- Introduce characters and context plainly (who they were, what was happening).
- Lean heavily on analogies and picture-painting.
- Use simple, clear, easy-to-understand language.
- Keep the same core ideas as the Normal version — just bring them down to the reader's level.

The aim is that a complete newcomer can follow the argument without ever feeling lost.

---

## Summary

1. **Normal first** — write the essay freely.
2. **Advanced** — re-read the resources, add evidential depth and verses, present it for scholars.
3. **Beginner** — assume no Bible knowledge, explain the stories and characters, keep it simple.
4. **British spelling** — in all three, always.
