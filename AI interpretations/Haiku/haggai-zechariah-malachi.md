# Haggai, Zechariah & Malachi: God's Character Revealed

*(This recording covers Haggai, Zechariah, and Malachi in two sessions.)*

## The Later Temple Was More Glorious — Because God Came Gently

Haggai 2:9 — "The latter splendor of this house shall be greater than the former." How could the modest post-exilic temple be more glorious than Solomon's?

- Solomon's temple had fire from heaven, the glory cloud, overwhelming majesty. The second temple had none of that.
- But the Messiah walked gently through its precincts — teaching, healing, loving. **That** is what made it more glorious.
- God never wanted to come with fire and pomp and majesty. He always wanted to walk and talk with people as a friend. The fire and majesty are awesome but not friendly.
- "The most glorious thing the universe ever saw was the infinitely powerful one walking around so gently — not from weakness, but from incredible discipline and self-control."
- This is the way God will govern the new earth: gently, not from Sinai. "Blessed are the meek, for the kingdom is theirs."

**What this shows about God:** God's glory is not His power on display — it is His gentleness. The greatest revelation of God was not fire from heaven but the Messiah walking softly among people. God prefers to govern from gentleness, not from majesty.

## Not by Might Nor by Power, but by My Spirit (Zechariah 4:6)

- "Not by might nor by power, but by my Spirit" does not mean "not by *your* might, but by *God's* power." It means God Himself says: not by *My* might or power, but by My Spirit — who works through love and truth.
- "Could God win because He has more power than the adversary? If that's all that needed to be displayed, how come it's taken thousands of years?"
- The conflict is not over who has power — the devil doesn't doubt God's power. The conflict is over who is telling the truth.
- "The infinitely powerful one, with all His might and power, could not hold the loyalty of Lucifer, who lived in His very presence. He lost him. Was that from lack of might or power? You can't produce loyalty by force."
- God could create a whole new universe by might and power, but He couldn't even win Judas by washing his feet.

**What this shows about God:** God does not win the great controversy by power. He wins by the Spirit — truth spoken in love. The most important things in the universe can only be won by demonstration of trustworthiness, never by force.

## God Defends His People Before the Universe (Zechariah 3)

Zechariah 3 is one of the key "heavenly court" passages — Joshua the high priest stands before the Angel of the Lord, and Satan accuses him.

- This is the same scene as Job 1–2 and Daniel 7: the family of the universe convened to discuss the affairs of the family. God does not conduct business behind closed doors.
- Satan is allowed to accuse — he recounts the sins. But Christ (the Angel of the Lord) defends, not by excusing the record but by testifying to a changed heart.
- "Everything the devil has said about brother Jones is true. In fact, he could have said more. But this is a new person — safe to have around."
- The record is never erased or hidden. God preserves it (Daniel 7:10 — "the books were opened"). We get in *in spite of* the record being there.
- The only question is: "Is this person safe to live next door to?" — not "Has this person been forgiven?" Cheats are not safe, whether forgiven or not.
- Christ does not say to Nicodemus "unless you are forgiven" but "unless you are born again" — a new heart, a right spirit, a willingness to listen.

**What this shows about God:** God defends His people before the watching universe — not by hiding their record but by testifying to real transformation. The investigative judgment is not a legal audit but the last family meeting, where Christ recommends those who have been genuinely changed as safe to admit.

## No One Is Needed Between Us and God

- "Do we need someone between us and God? Never. When God was here for three and a half years, was there anyone between the disciples and God?"
- We do need someone between us and the adversary — to hold off Satan's accusations. That is where Christ comes between.
- But between us and God? John 16:26 — "There is no need for me to intercede with the Father for you, for the Father loves you Himself."
- The idea of someone in between is an emergency measure for people who are scared of God. The people at Sinai begged for Moses to be in between. God wanted to speak to them directly.
- "God is very gracious and says: if you're so scared of me, you need to think of a friend in between — that's all right with me." Children think of Jesus as the friend between them and the awesome one. God is not jealous — He is willing to wait.
- But the one who came to bridge the gap *is* God.

**What this shows about God:** God has never wanted anyone between Himself and His people. The intercession of Christ is not to persuade a reluctant Father but to defend us against the adversary. The Father loves us Himself — He is as approachable as the Son.

## God's People Can Be Devout Without Knowing Him (Malachi)

Malachi is the tragic end of the Old Testament — God's people arguing with Him at every point.

- "I have loved you," says the Lord. "How have you loved us?" they reply.
- "You despise my name." "How have we despised your name?"
- "You have wearied the Lord with your words." "How have we wearied him?"
- "You have robbed me." "How have we robbed you?"
- They brought blind, lame, and sick animals for sacrifice — going through the motions without meaning it.
- They were unfaithful to the wives of their youth. They cheated in their dealings. They said "it is vain to serve God" and called the arrogant "blessed."
- Yet they were the most devout, blueprint-following people — keeping Sabbath, paying tithe (even of mint and cumin), straining out gnats, observing all the ceremonies.

**What this shows about God:** God is not honored by devout performance that lacks a knowing relationship. The most religious people can be the furthest from knowing Him. What God wants is not the ceremony but the heart — integrity, honesty, a willingness to listen.

## God Disciplines Those He Loves — They Remain the Apple of His Eye (Zechariah 2:8)

- "He who touches you touches the apple of His eye" — or, as the Living Bible puts it: "He who harms you sticks his finger in Jehovah's eye."
- God disciplines those He loves, but they remain the apple of His eye. Like a parent who disciplines a child in a private place — not to embarrass, not from hatred, but because the child is the apple of the parent's eye and must not turn out bad.
- The nations that carried Israel into captivity overdid it. "I was angry but a little with my people, and they furthered the disaster." God disciplines in turn those who were too harsh.

**What this shows about God:** God's discipline is not rejection. Even when He disciplines, His people remain the apple of His eye. He disciplines because He loves, not because He is angry — and He disciplines those who harm His people as well.

## God's Promises Are Conditional — History Is Often "What Might Have Been"

- Zechariah 6:15 — "This shall come to pass *if* you diligently obey the voice of the Lord your God."
- "The promises and the threatenings of God are like conditional." The later history of Israel is a history of what might have been.
- God promised prosperity, safety, and the nations coming to Jerusalem saying "God is with you" — but only if they obeyed. They didn't, so it didn't happen.
- This means prophetic promises of restoration are not unconditional guarantees — they depend on the response of God's people.

**What this shows about God:** God's promises are invitations, not guarantees. He offers restoration, but it is conditional on His people's willingness to listen. History could have been different — and God grieves over what might have been.

## God Waited Centuries — For What?

A key question: why did God bother rebuilding the temple when He knew His people would reject His Son?

- God was waiting for a group of people who had the Scriptures, who were not worshiping idols, who kept the Sabbath, who followed the ceremonies — so that when He came, there would be a context.
- For the first time in history, thanks to Ezra and Nehemiah, God had a group who accepted the 39 books, believed in creation, kept the Sabbath, followed the Ten Commandments and the sacrificial system.
- "He came to people who had never gone up into the mountains to sacrifice with cult prostitutes. They were the most pious, blueprint-following, Bible-quoting, Sabbath-keeping, tithing Adventists the world has ever known. And they didn't recognize Him."
- The application: "You can read the 39 books and come up with the devil's picture of God. They did it. Have we read it aright?"

**What this shows about God:** God waited centuries to give His people every advantage — and they still rejected Him. This warns every generation: having the right books, the right day, and the right rules does not guarantee we know God. The question is whether our picture of God matches the One Jesus revealed.

## God's Final Word Is Healing and Restoration (Malachi 4)

- "For you who revere my name, the Sun of Righteousness shall rise with healing in its wings. You shall go forth leaping like calves from the stall." (Malachi 4:2)
- The wicked will be ashes — not eternally burning, but ashes under the soles of the feet.
- Elijah will be sent before the great day — God always sends a messenger before a crisis.
- The Old Testament ends with both warning and promise — like Revelation, it ends with fire, but also with healing for those who revere God's name.

**What this shows about God:** God's final word is healing. The wicked are ashes, not eternally tormented. For those who revere Him, the future is vigor, health, and joy — leaping like calves released from the stall.

## Notes on What Is NOT Claimed

- The dating of these prophets (Haggai/Zechariah ~520 BC, Malachi ~425 BC) is acknowledged as approximate.
- The vivid imagery of Zechariah's eight visions (horsemen, horns, measuring line, golden lampstand, flying scroll, woman in basket, four chariots) is noted but not pressed into detailed interpretation — "everything is explained because Zechariah asked what it meant."
- The discussion of the 400 years between the testaments is flagged as a topic for the next session.
