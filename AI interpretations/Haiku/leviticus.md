# Leviticus: God's Character Revealed

## The Sanctuary System: God Teaching at the Sandbox

Leviticus begins after the tabernacle is built. The services begin: sacrifices, priests, ceremonies, rules, rituals, clean and unclean distinctions, and detailed instructions.

Maxwell compares this to God taking Israel to the **sandbox**. The people were not ready for plain, face-to-face friendship like Moses had. So God gave them a visible, concrete teaching system: a small building, rooms, furniture, priests, repeated services, and symbols.

**What this shows:**
- God is a patient and skillful teacher.
- God does not demand that people understand advanced truth before they are ready.
- God gives people forms they can grasp when they cannot yet understand plain speech.
- The symbols were not the goal; they were emergency teaching tools meant to lead people toward deeper understanding.

---

## Sacrifices: God Does Not Want Appeasement

The sacrifices were often misunderstood as if God enjoyed the smell of burning animals or needed blood to be appeased. Maxwell rejects that picture.

He points to Micah 6: God does not want thousands of rams, rivers of oil, or even the firstborn as payment for sin. God wants people to do justice, love kindness, and walk humbly with Him.

**What this shows:**
- God is not an offended deity who must be won over by blood.
- Sacrifice only had value if it helped bring people back to trust, humility, and willingness to listen.
- God wanted the sacrifices to teach the seriousness of sin and point forward to the solution He would provide.
- When sacrifice was used for the wrong reason, it supported a false picture of God.

---

## The Rules: God Gives Emergency Help, Not Arbitrary Tests

Leviticus contains many detailed rules. Maxwell argues these were not arbitrary tests of obedience. They were added because people needed help.

If people had stayed close to God, there would have been no need for Sinai, stone tablets, circumcision, or many added directions. The rules were emergency measures, carefully tailored to people's condition.

**What this shows:**
- God is not controlling or arbitrary.
- God gives rules to protect, teach, and guide people who are losing their way.
- God would rather people do right because they understand and agree, but when they are not ready for that, He gives them necessary safeguards.

---

## Decency and Order: God Wants Clear-Minded Worship

Maxwell contrasts Leviticus with the surrounding fertility religions. Those religions involved drunkenness, cult prostitution, emotional frenzy, and immorality consecrated in the name of worship.

Leviticus required modesty, sobriety, order, reverence, and carefulness. Priests were to dress modestly. They were not to serve drunk. The services were structured and orderly.

**What this shows:**
- God is not like the gods of the surrounding nations.
- God does not want worship that degrades people or confuses the mind.
- God wants reverent, clear-minded interaction.
- God values order because He wants people able to understand, reason, and respond intelligently.

---

## Moses: The Friend Who Understood the Symbols

Moses helped give Israel the sanctuary system, but he did not need it in the same way the people did. He spoke with God face to face, as a man speaks to his friend.

This means the sanctuary system was not God's highest ideal. It was a gracious provision for people who were not ready for the direct relationship Moses had.

**What this shows:**
- God's ideal is direct friendship and plain speech.
- When people are not ready for that, God provides symbols, rituals, priests, and ceremonies.
- God meets people at their level without abandoning the higher goal.

---

## Overall Leviticus Picture

Leviticus can look like a book of excessive detail, blood, rules, and ritual. But in Maxwell's reading, it reveals God as a wise, patient, versatile teacher.

God uses the sanctuary system because the people need concrete help. He gives rules because they need protection. He requires order because He wants clear-minded worship. He gives symbols because they cannot yet handle plain speech. But His goal remains friendship: people who understand Him, trust Him, and listen willingly.
